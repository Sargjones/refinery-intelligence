How Crude Oil Becomes Fuel — The Full Chain
Most people never see how raw crude oil becomes the gasoline in their car, the jet fuel in a plane, or the asphalt on a road. Each step happens in a specific place, often hundreds or thousands of kilometres apart, connected by pipelines, tankers, and trucks. A disruption anywhere in this chain propagates downstream within days to weeks.
Why This Matters Right Now

The Hormuz closure has blocked ~20% of global oil trade at Step 1 (extraction/export). But the damage cascades:

  • Refineries configured for Gulf crude cannot easily switch feedstock
  • North America's refinery base is already at 92%+ utilization
  • 7 major US refinery closures since 2019 left no spare throughput
  • Jet fuel is the most exposed product — only 21 days supply in 2026
  • Fertilizer (Step 4 byproduct) impacts food prices within one growing season
